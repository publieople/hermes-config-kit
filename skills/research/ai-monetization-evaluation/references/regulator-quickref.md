# Regulator & platform one-line ref

The single rule each authority actually enforces, with source. Use when you need to quote a specific barrier in a verdict.

## US financial regulators

| Body | Class it regulates | One rule that bites "AI auto-赚钱" | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CFTC** | Retail forex, futures, derivatives, crypto fraud | Customer Advisory *"AI Won't Turn Trading Bots into Money Machines"* — claims of AI-driven guaranteed returns = fraud red flag. CFTC Forex stats: ~2/3 retail FX traders lose per quarter | https://www.cftc.gov/PressRoom/PressReleases/8854-24 ; https://www.cftc.gov/LearnAndProtect/forexfrauds |
| **SEC** | Investment advice, robo-advisers, broker-dealers | "Investment tool" is fine; "investment advice" requires RIA registration. FY2025 exam priorities explicitly call out AI automated investment tools | https://www.sec.gov/files/2025-exam-priorities.pdf ; https://www.sec.gov/investment/im-guidance-2017-02.pdf |
| **FTC** | Endorsement / sponsorship disclosure (US) | Affiliate content must have clear material-connection disclosure. No `#ad` if you got paid for the link | https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/advertising-and-marketing/endorsements |

## Crypto / Web3 exchanges — KYC hurdle by jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Typical hurdle for an individual to "AI-trade autonomously" |
|---|---|
| US (federal) | FinCEN MSB state-by-state licensing often required for products interacting with fiat on behalf of users; exchange-side KYC blocks under-18, sanctioned regions |
| EU (MiCA effective 2024+) | CASP registration for crypto-asset service providers; AI-trading advisories subject to MiFID II if they constitute advice |
| Mainland China | Crypto trading accounts effectively blocked for individuals; exchanges left the market in 2021 |
| Hong Kong | SFC VATP regime for virtual asset trading platforms (Jun 2023+); retail trading allowed on licensed platforms only |
| Singapore | MAS DPT regulations + payment-services licensing for any product touching fiat on behalf of users |

When the user wants to actually take money out through any of these channels, ask their country of residence and age before recommending.

## Quick-check questions before granting authorization on any identity-bound step

- Is the user 18+ and resident in a jurisdiction where the target is legal?
- Does the user already hold the required identity document (passport, ID, address proof)?
- Does the user have a tax filing obligation in that jurisdiction?
- For crypto: does the user understand the irreversibility of on-chain mistakes and the key-management responsibility?

Any "no" → refuse the action, suggest the legal/research-only path.
